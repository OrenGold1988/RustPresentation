OpenTitan: First Commercial Open-Source RoT Chip

OpenTitan represents a paradigm shift in silicon security, moving away from "black-box" proprietary designs to a fully transparent Root of Trust.
As the world's first commercially available open-source hardware project, it provides a high-quality logical design that is independently verifiable.

Open Source Hardware addresses the following critical issues:
•
Vendor Neutrality and Supply Chain Resilience: Open-source hardware prevents "Vendor Lock-in." If a proprietary chip manufacturer goes out of business or changes its terms, the customer is no longer stuck with unsupported silicon.
•
Cost Reduction and Innovation: Building a Root of Trust from scratch is incredibly expensive. Open-source hardware allows companies to share the massive R&D costs of the "non-differentiating" security foundations, accelerating time-to-market.
